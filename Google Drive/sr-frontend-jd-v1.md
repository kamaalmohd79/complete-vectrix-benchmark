## About Vectrix

Vectrix is an API observability platform for mid-market SaaS companies. We help engineering teams debug API issues before customers notice. Think Datadog for APIs, but purpose-built and cheaper. We're 10 people, seed-funded ($2.4M from Threshold Ventures), growing fast, and in the middle of our most important customer deal to date.

We're remote-first, mostly async, and take craft seriously. Our stack: Next.js 14 / TypeScript / Tailwind CSS on the frontend, Go on the backend, ClickHouse for analytics, Kafka for ingestion.

---

## The Role

We need a Senior Frontend Engineer to own the dashboard experience. Sofia Gutierrez (our current frontend engineer) built the foundation, but we're now in a full v2 redesign — customizable widgets, saved views, real-time percentile charts — and we need more horsepower.

You'll work closely with Raj Sundaram (designer) and Dana Reeves (Head of Product). Marcus (CTO) reviews all major architectural decisions.

---

## What You'll Do

- Own the Dashboard v2 frontend from design handoff to production
- Build real-time data visualization components (WebSocket-fed latency charts, percentile toggles, anomaly overlays)
- Lead the frontend side of our auth migration (Firebase → Clerk)
- Contribute to the Next.js 14 app architecture — routing, data-fetching strategy, component design
- Collaborate with Raj on making the Figma designs actually buildable
- Write TypeScript that future engineers won't hate

---

## What We're Looking For

- 4+ years of production frontend experience
- Strong Next.js and TypeScript — you understand how App Router actually works
- Experience building real-time UIs (WebSockets, SSE, optimistic updates)
- Comfort with data visualization (recharts, D3, or similar)
- You have opinions about accessibility and performance, not just features
- Can work autonomously; we don't have a large team to hold your hand

**Nice to have:**

- Experience with Tailwind CSS at scale
- Familiarity with Clerk or Firebase Auth
- Background in developer tools or observability products
